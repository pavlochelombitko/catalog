Public Finance I.

Quick Review — Main Points Only

1 What is Finance?

The study of money, banking, credit, investments, assets and liabilities. Three main branches: public, corporate, and personal finance.

2 Public Finance

Public Finance = how governments raise money, spend it, and the effects on economy & society.
Monetary relations between government levels AND between government and other entities
Tied to the existence and evolution of the state

3 Why does the Public Sector exist?

Market failures → the private market can't provide everything efficiently
Public sector is funded by taxes (obligation principle)
When both market AND state fail → non-profit sector steps in

4 Public Goods

Two key properties: Non-rival (your use doesn't reduce mine) + Non-excludable (can't prevent anyone from using it)
RivalNon-rival
ExcludablePrivate (ice cream)Impure public (cable TV)

6 ESA 2010 — Public Sector Structure

EU accounting framework. Groups the economy into 5 institutional sectors. Government = S.13.
General Government = non-market producers, funded by taxes (market revenue < 50% of costs)
4 subsectors: central gov, state gov (federal countries only), local gov, social security funds

7 Federal vs. Unitary Systems

Federal

  • Sovereignty is divided
  • Lower units keep their powers
  • DE, AT, US, CA, CH, IN

Unitary

  • All power at national level
  • Local power is delegated (& revocable)
  • SK, UK, FR, HU, JP, SE
Fiscal federalism = which functions should be centralized vs. decentralized?

8 Managing Public Finance

R = E balanced  |  R > E surplus  |  R < E deficit
Deficit = government spends more than it collects → borrows → adds to national debt
Public debt = central gov debt + local gov debt + para-fiscal funds debt (always > national debt)

9 EU Debt Overview

EU Maastricht threshold: debt should be ≤ 60% of GDP
Many countries exceed it: Greece (~154%), Italy (~135%), France (~113%)
Slovakia 2024: debt = 59.7% GDP, deficit = -5.5%
